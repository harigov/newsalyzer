Here’s our analysis of the Republican debate, and our fact checks._______The field is shrinking, but the stakes are getting higher. The five remaining Republican presidential candidates will gather in Houston on Thursday night for their final debate before Super Tuesday on March 1.With Donald J. Trump having won three of the first four states, the pressure will be on Senators Marco Rubio and Ted Cruz to blunt his momentum. For Gov. John Kasich of Ohio and Ben Carson, the battle will be to keep their campaigns afloat.Here’s how to follow the action:The debate begins at 8:30 p.m. Eastern time and will be broadcast on CNN, CNN International and CNN en Español. It will also be translated live on Telemundo, a Spanish-language channel.CNN will stream the debate live on its website and mobile platforms with no login needed. Telemundo.com and Telemundo News’s YouTube channel will also stream the event.Be sure to follow along with The Times for up-to-the-minute fact-checking, analysis and our live chat.Facebook and Twitter will be full of real-time reaction, and the hashtag #GOPDebate is a handy way to find the conversation.Salem Media Group will broadcast the event on its stations.
