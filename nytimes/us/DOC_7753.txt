Election Day Information for New York, New Jersey and Connecticut - The New York Times
Voter Registration and Requirements,Board of Elections (NYC)
Nov. 3, 2014
7753
Polls in New York State are open on Tuesday from 6 a.m. to 9 p.m. Voters will elect the governor, lieutenant governor, attorney general and comptroller and decide three ballot questions. There are also races for Congress, the State Legislature and local offices. Voter information is available from the State Board of Elections at vote-ny.com or by calling 1-800-FOR-VOTE (1-800-367-8683); from the New York City Board of Elections at vote.nyc.ny.us or by calling 1-866-VOTE-NYC (1-866-868-3692), or from the New York Public Interest Research Group at 212-822-0282. Voters who have problems can visit Election Protection at 866ourvote.org or call 1-866-OUR-VOTE (1-866-687-8683).To check if you are registered to vote in New York and find out which districts you live in, visit voterlookup.elections.state.ny.us. Polling locations can be found at nyc.pollsitelocator.com by typing in your address.Connecticut polls are open from 6 a.m. to 8 p.m. Voters will elect the governor, lieutenant governor, secretary of the state, treasurer, comptroller, attorney general, members of Congress and state legislators, along with local officials, and decide one ballot question. Voters can look up their voter registration information and polling location on dir.ct.gov. Information is also available by calling 1-866-SEEC-INFO (1-866-733-2463), or by email at elections@ct.gov. Information about registering to vote on Election Day can be found on ct.gov.New Jersey polls are also open from 6 a.m. to 8 p.m. Voters will elect a United States senator and members of Congress and decide two ballot questions. Voters can view their voter registration information at voter.njsvrs.com, and find their individual polling location at voter.njsvrs.com/elections/polling-lookup.html. More information is available at njelections.org or by calling 1-877-NJ-VOTER (1-877-658-6837).
