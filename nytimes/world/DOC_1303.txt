This article was updated on June 6, 2017, to reflect the increased death toll.Afghan officials updated the death toll of a devastating terror attack in the capital Kabul on May 31, saying 150 people were killed and more than 300 wounded, in a blast that shattered windows as far as a mile away.Residents described hearing an enormous explosion as a mushroom cloud rose over the car bomb site, enveloping the city in acrid black smoke. The authorities said a truck laden with explosives left a crater five yards wide.The immediate blast area is visible in these photos from Planet, a satellite imagery company:The target of the explosion was Zanbaq Square in the Wazir Akbar Khan neighborhood, a bustling area of the capital near the presidential palace and many foreign embassies.The explosion took place at the peak of rush hour in Kabul, when the streets were packed with residents making their way to work.The German Embassy was heavily damaged in the explosion. Officials from the German, Japanese and Pakistani embassies said their staff members had been injured in the attack.After the attack, the roads in Wazir Akbar Khan were sealed off by the Afghan security forces and helicopters were seen hovering overhead.Residents and rescue works swarmed the area, offering first aid and taking the injured away in ambulances and cars.
